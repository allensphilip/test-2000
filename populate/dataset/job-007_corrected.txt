Seventeen year old male presents to sports medicine clinic with right knee pain following basketball injury three days ago. Mechanism of injury involved sudden pivoting movement with audible pop. Immediate swelling developed within two hours. Patient reports knee instability with giving way sensation. Unable to bear full weight without pain. Physical examination reveals moderate joint effusion. Positive Lachman test and anterior drawer test suggesting anterior cruciate ligament injury. McMurray test negative. No medial or lateral joint line tenderness. Range of motion limited by pain and swelling. MRI ordered to assess ligament and meniscal integrity. Knee immobilizer provided for comfort. Crutches dispensed with non weight bearing instructions. NSAIDs prescribed for pain and inflammation. Referral to orthopedic surgery for evaluation and treatment planning. Discussion regarding surgical versus conservative management pending imaging results.
