Forty one year old female with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease presents with acute exacerbation. Symptoms include increased dyspnea productive cough with purulent sputum and wheezing for past three days. Patient reports twenty pack year smoking history currently smoking half pack per day. Home medications include tiotropium eighteen micrograms daily and albuterol as needed. Physical examination reveals diffuse expiratory wheezing bilaterally. Oxygen saturation ninety percent on room air improved to ninety five percent on two liters nasal cannula. Chest radiograph shows hyperinflation without acute infiltrate. Spirometry demonstrates FEV1 fifty five percent predicted. Treated with nebulized albuterol ipratropium and oral prednisone forty milligrams daily for five days. Azithromycin prescribed for possible bacterial superinfection. Strong smoking cessation counseling provided with referral to cessation program. Follow up in pulmonary clinic two weeks.
