Twenty four year old female presents with symptoms of urinary tract infection including dysuria frequency and urgency for two days. Patient reports suprapubic discomfort and cloudy urine. No fever or flank pain. No vaginal discharge. Not sexually active currently. No history of recurrent UTIs. Urinalysis shows positive leukocyte esterase and nitrites. Microscopy reveals greater than fifty white blood cells per high power field. Urine culture sent to laboratory. Diagnosed with acute uncomplicated cystitis. Prescribed nitrofurantoin one hundred milligrams twice daily for five days. Educated regarding increased fluid intake and complete antibiotic course. Advised to follow up if symptoms persist beyond seventy two hours or develop fever. Discussed prevention strategies including post coital voiding and adequate hydration. Follow up culture not needed if symptoms resolve completely.
