Twenty eight year old male presents to emergency department with acute chest pain radiating to left arm. Pain began approximately one hour ago while exercising at gymnasium. Described as crushing sensation with severity eight out of ten. Associated with diaphoresis and shortness of breath. No previous cardiac history. Family history significant for myocardial infarction in father at age fifty five. ECG shows ST segment elevation in leads two three and AVF. Troponin I elevated at twelve point four nanograms per milliliter. Activated cardiac catheterization laboratory. Aspirin three hundred twenty five milligrams administered. Clopidogrel loading dose six hundred milligrams given. Heparin bolus initiated. Patient transferred to interventional cardiology for emergency percutaneous coronary intervention. Prognosis discussed with family members present at bedside.
